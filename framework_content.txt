Executive Summary The Consumer Persona & Scenario Intelligence Framework is a unified analytical layer that converts raw operational data — from smart meters, billing systems, payment records, CRM, and consumer apps — into actionable consumer-level intelligence. Every consumer receives six scores monthly. These scores are combined into personas. Personas drive targeted, differentiated actions across six operational domains: collections, DSM, field operations, customer experience, network planning, and regulatory risk management. What This Framework Enables Predict and prevent payment defaults before they occur — reducing field enforcement cost by 30–40% Route defaulters correctly: digital recovery vs field visit based on engagement level Target DSM and Demand Response programmes at consumers with genuine flexible load Identify consumers whose DT/feeder stress can be resolved by behaviour change vs infrastructure upgrade Pre-empt billing complaints and regulatory escalations before they leave the DISCOM Personalise communications by channel, timing, and message based on digital readiness 2. Framework Architecture The framework operates in four sequential layers: Layer 1 Raw Data Layer 2 Attributes Layer 3 Scores Layer 4 Personas → Actions Layer 5 LLM Explainability Data is refreshed monthly, aligned to the billing cycle. Each consumer holds one analytical record per billing month, enabling month-on-month trend tracking and intervention effectiveness measurement. 3. The Six Scoring Dimensions Every consumer receives all six scores each billing month. Persona assignment and action routing are driven entirely by combinations of these scores. No score is used in isolation. What triggers the flag: An existing complaint has not been resolved within 30 days — the consumer now has the legal right to approach the Ombudsman or State Consumer Forum The CRM escalation flag is already set, meaning the consumer has explicitly threatened or initiated external escalation Complaint Risk Score is above 70 AND average resolution TAT exceeds 15 days — a predictive trigger before escalation occurs Complaint is in billing or metering category AND no revised bill has been issued within the investigation window Escalation chain: DISCOM internal helpline → DISCOM Ombudsman → State Consumer Forum (Consumer Protection Act) → SERC (tariff / supply grievances) Why it is different from Complaint Risk Score: Complaint Risk Score measures the probability of any complaint being raised. The Regulatory Risk Flag is narrower — it measures the probability of that complaint leaving the DISCOM's control and entering a regulatory or legal forum. A consumer can have a high complaint risk score but a clear regulatory flag if complaints are being resolved quickly. Complaint Risk Score = volume risk. Regulatory Risk Flag = institutional / legal risk. 4. Persona Library — All 18 Personas Each consumer is assigned a primary persona and, where applicable, a secondary persona each billing month. Conclusion & Next Steps The Consumer Persona & Scenario Intelligence Framework gives every team in the DISCOM a single, consistent view of each consumer — updated every billing month — with a clear recommended action attached. The framework is explainable (every persona is traceable to specific score inputs), regulator-safe (no black-box AI), and scalable (monthly batch processing on existing data infrastructure). Recommended Implementation Phases Phase 1 — Data foundation: Implement the 6-table Consumer 360 data model. Validate attribute extraction from MDMS, billing, payment, CRM, and app systems. Phase 2 — Scoring engine: Build the 6 scoring algorithms. Run shadow scoring for 2 billing cycles to validate distributions and thresholds. Phase 3 — Persona assignment: Deploy rule-based persona assignment. Integrate with CRM and collections workflow for action routing. Phase 4 — LLM explainability layer: Connect analytical outputs to LLM-based query interface for business teams to interrogate consumer, DT, feeder, and area-level insights in natural language. Phase 5 — Feedback loop: Measure KPIs monthly. Refine thresholds based on actual intervention outcomes. Add secondary persona routing. For questions on implementation, data model specifications, or scenario prioritisation, contact the Revenue Assurance & CX Strategy team.
